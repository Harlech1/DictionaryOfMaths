paranthesis pl.\ pl. parantheses

I. A paranthesis is used to denote an open end of an interval.

II. Parantheses are used to denote

(a) modifications to normal order of operations or to clarify order of operations,

(b) functions in the form f(x)f(x), which means that values of ff are dependent upon the values of xx,

(c) the greatest common divisor gcd(a, b) or lowest common multiple lcm(a, b),

(d) the ordered n-tuple of numbers (x,y), (x,y,z) etc.,

(e) a matrix (large parantheses around an array of numbers indicate a matrix),

(f) binomial coeficients (large parantheses around two numbers, one above the other indicate combinations), and

(g) a congruence, as in a≡b(modm)a \equiv b\ (mod\ m).
