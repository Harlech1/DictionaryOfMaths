logic.logic. conditional proposition; implication

If pp\ and qq\ are statements, then the implication p⇒qp \Longrightarrow q is the assertion that pp\ implies qq.

If the implication p⇒qp \Longrightarrow q is true and if pp\ happens to be true , then qq\ is also true .

Statement pp\ is called the hypothesis (or premise) and statement qq\ is called the conclusion of the implication.
