orthogonal matrices

A square matrix is orthogonal iff ATA=AAT=IA^{T}A = AA^{T} = I, where ATA^{T}is the transpose of AA and II is the identity matrix.

The determinant of an orthogonal matrix is either 1 or −-1.

An orthogonal matrix is always invertible, and A−1=ATA^{- 1} = A^{T}.

The product of two orthogonal matrices is another orthogonal matrix.

The inverse of an orthogonal matrix is also orthogonal.
