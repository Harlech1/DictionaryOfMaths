Interchanging two rows (or columns) of a determinant yields a determinant that is the negative of the original determinant.

If entries of two rows (or columns) of a determinant are proportional , then the determinant is equal to zero.

Interchanging the rows of a determinant with its columns does not change its value.

If any row (or column) of a determinant is multiplied by a nonzero multiplier kk , then the resulting determinant

is kk times the original determinant.

Scalars can be factored out from rows and columns.

If a multiple of any row (or column) of a determinant is added to any row (or column), then the value of the

determinant is the same as that of the original determinant.

The value of a triangular determinant is the product of the elements of the principal diagonal.

Any determinant with two rows (or columns) equal has value 0.

If one of rows (or columns) of a determinant consists of zeros, then the determinant has value 0.
