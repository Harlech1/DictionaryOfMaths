parallel vectors

Two vectors of nonzero length are parallel if they are multiples of each other,i.e., u→\overrightarrow{u} =kv→= k\overrightarrow{v} ⇔\Leftrightarrow u→\overrightarrow{u} // v→\overrightarrow{v}, wherek∈R\ k \in R.

In R2:R^{2}: u→\overrightarrow{u} =(x1,y1)= \left( x_{1},y_{1} \right) // v→\overrightarrow{v} = (x2,y2)(x_{2},y_{2}) ⇒\Longrightarrow x1x2=y1y2=k,\frac{x_{1}}{x_{2}} = \frac{y_{1}}{y_{2}} = k\ ,\ (k∈R)(k \in R)

In R3:R^{3}: u→\overrightarrow{u} =(x1,y1,z1)//v→= \left( x_{1},y_{1},z_{1} \right)//\overrightarrow{v} = (x2,y2,z2)(x_{2},y_{2},z_{2}) ⇒\Longrightarrow x1x2=y1y2=z1z2=k\ \frac{x_{1}}{x_{2}} = \frac{y_{1}}{y_{2}} = \frac{z_{1}}{z_{2}} = k, (k∈R)(k \in R)
