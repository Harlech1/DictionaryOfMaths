binomial theorem

A general formula which tells how to expand the integer powers of a binomial without performing repeated

multiplications.

(a+b)n(a + b)^{n} =(n0)an+(n1)an−1b+(n2)an−2b2+(n3)an−3b3+…+(nn)bn=∑k=0nn!k!(n−k)!an−kbk= \left( \begin{array}{r} n \\ 0 \end{array} \right)a^{n} + \left( \begin{array}{r} n \\ 1 \end{array} \right)a^{n - 1}b + \left( \begin{array}{r} n \\ 2 \end{array} \right)a^{n - 2}b^{2} + \left( \begin{array}{r} n \\ 3 \end{array} \right)a^{n - 3}b^{3} + \ldots + \left( \begin{array}{r} n \\ n \end{array} \right)b^{n} = \sum_{k = 0}^{n}{\frac{n!}{k!(n - k)!}a^{n - k}b^{k}}

(a−b)n(a - b)^{n} =(n0)an−(n1)an−1b+(n2)an−2b2−(n3)an−3b3…+(−1)n(nn)bn=∑k=0n(−1)n(nk)an−kbk= \left( \begin{array}{r} n \\ 0 \end{array} \right)a^{n} - \left( \begin{array}{r} n \\ 1 \end{array} \right)a^{n - 1}b + \left( \begin{array}{r} n \\ 2 \end{array} \right)a^{n - 2}b^{2} - \left( \begin{array}{r} n \\ 3 \end{array} \right)a^{n - 3}b^{3}\ldots + ( - 1)^{n}\left( \begin{array}{r} n \\ n \end{array} \right)b^{n} = \sum_{k = 0}^{n}{( - 1)^{n}\left( \begin{array}{r} n \\ k \end{array} \right)a^{n - k}b^{k}}

where n is any positive integer.

The expansion of (a+b)n(a + b)^{n} has n+1n + 1 terms. The sum of the exponents of aa and bb in each term is nn.
