power of the point with respect to (relative to) the circle

The power of a point PP\ with respect to a circle of radius rr and center OO is defined by

p=|PA||PB|=|PO|2−r2p = |PA||PB| = |PO|^{2} - r^{2} where AA\ and BB are the intersection points of a line through PP with the circle.

I. If PP is outside the circle, its power is positive and equal to the square of the length of the segment of the

tangent from PP to the circle.

II. If PP\ is on the circumference of the circle, its power is 0.

III. If PP is inside the circle, its power is negative.
