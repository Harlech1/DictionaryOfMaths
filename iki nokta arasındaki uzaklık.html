distance between two points; point-point distance

The distance between two points is the length of the path connecting them.

In R2:R^{2}:\ The distance between the points A(x1,y1)A(x_{1},y_{1}) and B(x2,y2)B(x_{2},y_{2}) is |AB|=(x2−x1)2+(y2−y1)2|AB| = \ \sqrt{\left( x_{2} - x_{1} \right)^{2} + \left( y_{2} - y_{1} \right)^{2}}.

In R3:R^{3}:\ The distance between the points A(x1,y1,z1)A(x_{1},y_{1},z_{1}) and B(x2,y2,z2)B(x_{2},y_{2},z_{2}) is

|AB|=(x2−x1)2+(y2−y1)2+(z2−z1)2|AB| = \sqrt{\left( x_{2} - x_{1} \right)^{2} + \left( y_{2} - y_{1} \right)^{2} + \left( z_{2} - z_{1} \right)^{2}} .
