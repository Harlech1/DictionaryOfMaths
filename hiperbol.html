hyperbola

1.A hyperbola is the set of all points in the plane, the difference of whose distances from two fixed points is a given positive constant. The two fixed points are called the foci of the hyperbola.

2. A hyperbola is the set of all points in the plane, whose distance from a fixed point (the focus) is proportional to the horizontal distance from a fixed vertical line (the directrix), where the ratio is greater than Hyperbolas have two distinct foci and two associated directrices, each directrix being perpendicular to the line joining the two foci.
