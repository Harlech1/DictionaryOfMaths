compound interest

If the interest due at the end of a payment period is added to the principal and the interest computed at the end of the next payment period is based on this new principal amount ( old principal + interest ), then the interest is said to have been compounded.

PP_{}: capital (principal invested , deposited or borrowed) ; present value of the account

r:r: yearly rate of interest nn: principal compounded n times per year t: number of years the amount is invested

A:A: the total amount after t years ; future value of the account A=P.(1+rn)ntA = P.\left( 1 + \frac{r}{n} \right)^{nt}
