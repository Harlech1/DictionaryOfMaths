mutually exclusive events

Two events are mutually exclusive if they can not occur at the same time.

If events AA andBB are disjoint so that P(A∩B)=⌀P(A \cap B) = \varnothing, we say that they are mutually exclusive.

In this case, P(A∩B)=0P(A \cap B) = 0 and P(A∪B)=P(A)+P(B)P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B).
