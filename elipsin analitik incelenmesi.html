analytic study of the ellipse

An ellipse is the set of all points in the plane sum of whose distances from two fixed points is a given positive constant

that is greater than the distance between the fixed points. The two fixed points are called the foci of the ellipse.

An ellipse can also be defined as the locus of points whose distance from a fixed point (locus) is proportional to the

horizontal distance from a fixed line (directrix), where the ratio is less than 1.
