properties of the parallelogram

A parallelogram has no symmetry. Opposite sides are parallel and they have the same length.

Opposite angles are congruent and adjacent angles are supplementary.

Bisectors of two adjacent interior angles are perpendicular. Bisectors of opposite angles are parallel to each other.

The quadrilateral formed by the angle bisectors is a rectangle. The diagonals bisect each other.

Each diagonal seperates the paralleogram into two triangular regions with equal areas.

If we connect, in order, the midpoints of the sides of a parallelogram, then we form another parallelogram.
