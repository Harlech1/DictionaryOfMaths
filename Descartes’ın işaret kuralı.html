Descartes’ sign rule

A method for determining the maximum number of positive and negative real roots of a polynomial P(x)P(x).

I. For positive roots , rearrange the polynomial in ascending (or descending) powers of xx. Starting with the leading coefficient count the number of sign changes nn of the coefficients as you proceed . Then the number of allowable positive roots is n,n−2,n−4,...n,\ n - 2,\ n - 4,\ \ \ .\ \ .\ \ . .So nn is the maximum number of positive real roots.

II. For negative roots, write the polynomial P(−x)P( - x) and proceed as before to count the number of sign changes nn which is the maximum number of negative real roots of P(x)P(x).
