permutations of distinct objects without repetition

The symbol P(n,r)P(n,r) , read as ‘’ the number of permutations of nn distinct objects taken rr at a time’’ , denotes the number of different ordered arrangements of rr objects chosen from nn distinct objects , where 0≤r≤n0 \leq r \leq n and repetition is not allowed (once an object is used it can not be repeated in an arrangement).

P(n,r)=n!(n−r)!=n(n−1)(n−2)...(n−r+1)P(n,r) = \frac{n!}{(n - r)!} = n(n - 1)(n - 2)\ \ .\ \ .\ \ .\ \ (n - r + 1), where 0≤r≤n0 \leq r \leq n.

P(n,n)=n!P(n,n) = n! , P(n,0)=1P(n,\ 0) = 1 and P(n,1)=nP(n,1) = n.
