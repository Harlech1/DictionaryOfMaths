factorial notation

The factorial n!n! gives the number of ways in which nn objects can be permuted.

As nn grows large, factorials begin acquiring tails of trailing zeros.

0!=10! = 1 n!=n[(n−1)!]n! = n\left\lbrack (n - 1)! \right\rbrack n!=n(n−1)[(n−2)!]n! = n(n - 1)\left\lbrack (n - 2)! \right\rbrack

The only known factorials which are products of factorials in an arithmetic sequence are:
