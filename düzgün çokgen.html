regular polygon

An n-sided convex polygon having all its angles congruent and all its sides congruent.

It is both equilateral and equiangular. Its sides are symetrically placed about a common center.

The perpendicular bisectors of the sides and the angle bisectors of a regular polygon meet at the same point

which is both the center of the incircle and the center of the circumcircle .

By connecting every other vertex of a regular hexagon, we form an equilateral triangle.

By connecting every other vertex of a regular octagon, we form a square.
