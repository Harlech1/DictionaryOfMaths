finding (forming) the inverse function

To find the inverse of a given function, if it exists, we interchange its domain and range.

I. If the function is defined by a set of ordered pairs, then we interchange the positions of the first and second members of each ordered pair to form its inverse.

II. If the function is defined by an equation, then we form its inverse by interchanging xx and yy, and then solving for yy in terms of xx.
