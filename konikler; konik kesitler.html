conics; conic section

Curves obtained as intersections of a plane with a double-napped circular cone are called conic sections or conics.

If the plane passes through the vertex of the double-napped cone, then the intersection is a point, a pair of intersecting lines, or a single line. These are called degenerate conic sections. The conics are:

a) Circles , when the plane is perpendicular to the axis of the cone and intersects each generator.

b) Ellipses, when the plane is tilted so that it intersects each generator, but intersects only one nappe of the cone.

c) Hyperbolas, when the plane intersects both nappes.

D) Parabolas , when the plane is tilted farther so that it is parallel to one and only one generator and intersects only one nappe of the cone.
