spherical frustum; spherical segment

Spherical segment is a solid bounded by two parallel planes through a sphere. Area of the lower base is A1=πr12A_{1} = \pi{r_{1}}^{2};

area of the upper base is A1=πr22A_{1} = \pi{r_{2}}^{2}; area of the zone (curved surface) is Az=2πRhA_{z} = 2\pi Rh; total area of the segment

AT=2πrh+πr12+πr22A_{T} = 2\pi rh + \pi{r_{1}}^{2} + \pi{r_{2}}^{2} and volume of the segment is V=πh6(3r12+3r22+h2)V = \frac{\pi h}{6}(3{r_{1}}^{2} + 3{r_{2}}^{2} + h^{2}), where r1r_{1} and r2r_{2} are the radii of

the bases of the segment , RR is the radius of the sphere and hh is the height of the segment.

The surface of the spherical segment (bases are not included) is called spherical zone.
